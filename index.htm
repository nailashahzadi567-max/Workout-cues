1.     PUSHUPS (3 Sets of 6-10):

Week 1-2(10 wall), Week 3-4(10 inclined at belly button height), Week 5,6(10 knee pushups), Week 7(10 negative+knee), Week 8(proper pushups(initial 6))

 

Keep your eyes focused on the floor about 1 to 2 feet in front of your hands to avoid jutting head.

Wall pushups: Stand a few feet from a wall, placing palms(open fingers) flat at chest level. Maintain a rigid, straight line from your head to your heels, Engage your core, keep elbows at a 45-degree angle, and lower your chest toward the wall.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Incline push-up: Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width on the edge of the elevated surface. Grip firmly with your knuckles. Step your feet back so your body forms a completely straight diagonal line from the top of your head down to your ankles/heels. Brace your abdominals (draw your belly button inward) and squeeze your glutes firmly. This locks your pelvis and protects your lower back. Keep your neck in a neutral, straight alignment with your spine. Look slightly forward or a few inches ahead, rather than straight down at your feet or up at the ceiling. As you lower yourself, keep your elbows tucked at a roughly 45° angle from your torso. Avoid letting them flare out into a "T" shape, which strains the shoulders. Lower your torso until your chest lightly touches or comes within an inch of the elevated surface. Your shoulders should not drop below the height of your elbows.

Knee Pushups: Form a straight line from your head to your knees. Engage your core and glutes to keep your back flat, place hands shoulder-width apart, and lower your chest by tucking your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your torso.

 

OR

 

 

 

Negative Pushups:

Place your hands flat on the floor, slightly wider than shoulder-width, with your shoulders stacked directly over your wrists. Rise up onto your toes, straighten your legs, and keep your feet about hip-width apart. Squeeze your glutes and brace your abdominal muscles. As you begin to bend your arms, angle your elbows backward at roughly a 45-degree angle to your torso. Descend slowly and deliberately, counting to 3 to 5 seconds before your chest lightly touches the floor. Maintain total body tension and do not let gravity pull you down.

Once your chest gently touches the floor, you can drop your knees to the ground to take the tension off your upper body and reset posture for next rep.

Breathing & Tempo

  • Inhale: Lower your body in a slow, controlled manner.
  • *Exhale: Push back up through the palms of your hands until your arms are fully extended (but not locked).

 

2.     Band Rows (3 Sets of 12-15):

3.      

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Sit on the floor with your legs extended in front of you and knees slightly bent.
  2. Wrap the band around a piece of sturdy furniture and hold one end of the band in each hand. If nothing is available, you can wrap the band around the soles of your feet.
  3. Sit upright with your chest lifted and core engaged.
  4. Pull the band toward your torso by bending your elbows and drawing them backward.
  5. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement.
  6. Slowly extend your arms to return to the starting position.

 

Common mistakes: Shrugging shoulders, rounding back, using momentum.

Progression from easy to difficult:

Instead of

  • Pull → Return

Do

  • Pull in 2 seconds
  • Hold for 2,3 seconds
  • Lower in 4,5 seconds

Now even a lighter band becomes challenging.

Instead of both arms together:

  • Right arm 12 reps
  • Left arm 12 reps

One arm gets roughly twice the resistance.

Instead of

3 sets of 12, 15 reps

Do

  • 4 sets of 15, 20 reps

or

  • 3 sets of 20, 25 reps

if the resistance is still manageable

Shift to strongest band at last.

 

4.     Band Squats (3 Sets of 15 reps):

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Stand on the center of a resistance band with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out.
  2. Hold both ends of the band at your shoulders. Loop the band over each shoulder if it is long enough for stability.
  3. Stand tall. Brace your core. Lift your chest.
  4. Sit your hips back and down into a squat.
  5. Lower until your thighs are parallel or your hip crease drops below your knees.
  6. Drive through your full foot to stand. The band tension increases as you rise.
  7. Squeeze your glutes at the top against the peak band tension.

 

 

Common mistakes: Knees collapsing inward, heels lifting, rounding lower back.

 

Progression:

Add 1-2 reps to every time if all sets felt comfortable.

Keep excellent form; if able, add one extra set.

 

5.      Band Biceps Curl (2 sets of 12)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.      Step on the middle of the band. You can adjust the resistance by stepping wider or wrapping the band around your feet.

2.      Pull your shoulder blades back and down. Do not let your shoulders roll forward, as this shifts the work to your chest.

3.      Keep your elbows "glued" to your ribcage. Do not swing your elbows forward and backward; think of your elbow as a locked hinge.

4.      Squeeze your belly button in toward your spine to keep your back straight. Bend your knees slightly to create a strong, stable base.

5.      Curl your hands up to your shoulders. Squeeze the biceps at the top. Lower your hands slowly to the starting position.

6.      Keep your wrists straight. Bending them too far back or forward will make your forearm muscles take over the work

Common mistakes: Swinging body, elbows drifting forward.

Breathing: Exhale as you curl hands toward shoulders and inhale when you lower hands to starting position

Progression:

Add reps

Increase band resistance or sets

 

6.      Band Overhead Press (3 sets of 12-15)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.         Hold the water bottle firmly in your hand.

2.         Bring the bottle up to your shoulder.

3.         Bend your elbow at a 90 angle, pointing it forward.

4.         Stand with your feet shoulder width apart.

 

 

Progression: Increase reps or sets

 

7.     Glute Bridge (3 sets of15)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1.             Lie on your back. Keep feet flat on the floor, shoulder-width apart.

2.             Pull your belly button down.

3.             Your lower back should gently touch the floor.

4.             Push through your heels

5.             Lift your hips until your knees, hips, and shoulders make one straight line.

6.             Squeeze your glute muscles hard at the top. Do not arch your lower back.

7.             Slowly lower your hips back to the floor.

 

Progression:

Hold for some time and focus on keeping your bottom tight.

Increase hold time and reps.

 

 

8.     Plank (3 sets of 20-30 secs)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Lie down, keep feet hips width apart, rise with elbow, body should be in a straight line, tighten core and glute (as if taking a punch).

Level 1: Kneeling Plank

Get on your knees and forearms. Keep your back flat. Pull your belly button in.

Level 2: Standard Elbow Plank

Rest on your toes and forearms. Squeeze your glutes (butt muscles). This keeps your hips up. Imagine the floor as a wall; press your elbows down to activate your chest and shoulders.

 

Common mistakes: Hips too high/low, holding breath, neutral neck

Increase time
